Emergency Electrical Safety Checklist

Follow these critical steps when facing an electrical emergency to keep yourself and your family safe.

If You Smell Burning or See Smoke

  1. 1. Evacuate everyone from the building immediately
  2. 2. Call 911 from outside the building
  3. 3. Do not re-enter until fire department declares it safe

Power Outage

  1. 1. Check if the outage affects only your home or the neighborhood
  2. 2. If only your home, check your main electrical panel for tripped breakers
  3. 3. Reset any tripped breakers by firmly pushing to OFF position first, then to ON
  4. 4. If breakers continue to trip, call an emergency electrician
  5. 5. Use battery-powered lighting, not candles, during outages

Sparking Outlet or Switch

  1. 1. Turn off power to the circuit at your breaker panel immediately
  2. 2. Do not use the outlet or switch under any circumstances
  3. 3. If you smell burning, check for hot spots on the wall (careful, don't touch)
  4. 4. Call an emergency electrician to inspect and repair

Water Near Electrical Equipment

  1. 1. Never touch electrical devices if you're standing in water
  2. 2. If safe, turn off power at the main breaker panel
  3. 3. If you cannot safely reach the panel, call an emergency electrician
  4. 4. After flooding, have all electrical systems professionally inspected before turning power back on

Burning Smell from Electrical Source

  1. 1. Turn off power at the main electrical panel
  2. 2. Unplug any devices that might be causing the smell
  3. 3. If the smell persists or intensifies, evacuate and call 911
  4. 4. Do not turn power back on until an electrician has inspected your system

When to Call an Emergency Electrician

Some electrical issues require immediate professional attention. Don't delay if you experience any of these situations.

Electrical Shocks

If appliances or outlets deliver any shock, stop using them immediately and call a professional.

Burning Odors

A persistent burning smell from outlets, switches, or electrical panels requires immediate attention.

Sparking Outlets

Visible sparks from outlets or switches indicate a serious electrical problem that needs urgent repair.

Frequent Breaker Trips

If circuit breakers trip repeatedly after resetting, your electrical system is overloaded or failing.

Partial Power Loss

If parts of your home have no power while others do, you may have a dangerous partial service interruption.

Buzzing Sounds

Buzzing or humming from outlets, switches, or panels can indicate arcing electricity and fire hazards.

Preventing Electrical Emergencies

Take these proactive steps to reduce the risk of electrical emergencies in your home.

Regular Maintenance

  • Schedule annual electrical safety inspections
  • Test GFCI outlets monthly with the test button
  • Check smoke and carbon monoxide detectors regularly
  • Replace damaged or fraying power cords immediately
  • Keep electrical panel easily accessible

Safety Upgrades

  • Install AFCI protection to prevent electrical fires
  • Update old electrical panels (25+ years old)
  • Add whole-home surge protection
  • Replace aluminum wiring with copper
  • Upgrade to tamper-resistant outlets in homes with children

Creating a Family Electrical Emergency Plan

Prepare your household by creating and practicing an emergency response plan.

1. Know Your Electrical Panel

Ensure every adult and responsible teenager knows where the main electrical panel is located and how to turn off power to the entire house or individual circuits.

Pro Tip: Label all circuit breakers clearly with the areas or appliances they control. Keep a flashlight near the panel.

2. Create an Emergency Contact List

Post a list of important contacts in a visible location, including:

  • Emergency services (911)
  • AJ Long Electric emergency line: 703-997-0026
  • Local utility company emergency number
  • Poison control: 1-800-222-1222 (for electrical burns)
  • Trusted neighbor or family member

3. Establish Evacuation Routes

Plan and practice two ways to exit each room, especially in case of an electrical fire. Designate a meeting spot outside your home.

4. Prepare an Emergency Kit

Assemble a kit that includes:

  • Battery-powered flashlights (not candles)
  • Battery-powered radio
  • Extra batteries
  • First-aid kit with burn treatment
  • Insulated gloves for emergency electrical handling
  • Battery power banks for phones
