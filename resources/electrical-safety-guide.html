GFCI Outlet Requirements

Where GFCI Protection is Required

  • Bathrooms: All bathroom outlets must be GFCI protected
  • Kitchen Countertops: Within 6 feet of any sink
  • Outdoor Outlets: All exterior receptacles
  • Garages: All receptacles except those not readily accessible
  • Unfinished Basements: All outlets in unfinished areas
  • Crawl Spaces: All receptacles at or below grade level
  • Laundry Areas: Within 6 feet of sinks and washing machines

Testing Your GFCI Outlets

Monthly Testing Procedure:

  1. Plug in a small appliance or lamp
  2. Press the "TEST" button on the GFCI outlet
  3. The device should turn off immediately
  4. Press the "RESET" button
  5. The device should turn back on
  6. If it doesn't work properly, call an electrician

Home Electrical Safety Tips

Warning Signs

  • • Flickering or dimming lights
  • • Warm outlets or switch plates
  • • Burning smells
  • • Frequent circuit breaker trips
  • • Mild shock from appliances
  • • Discolored outlets

Water Safety

  • • Never touch electrical with wet hands
  • • Keep electrical devices away from water
  • • Use GFCI outlets in wet locations
  • • Install proper drainage around outdoor outlets
  • • Never use electrical devices in bathtubs

Outlet Safety

  • • Don't overload outlets
  • • Use outlet covers with children
  • • Replace damaged outlets immediately
  • • Unplug devices when not in use
  • • Never force plugs into outlets

Electrical Emergency Safety

⚡ Electrical Fire Emergency

  1. Turn off power at the breaker if safely possible
  2. Never use water on electrical fires
  3. Use a Class C fire extinguisher
  4. Call 911 immediately
  5. Evacuate if fire spreads
  6. Have electrical system inspected before restoring power

⚡ Electrical Shock Emergency

  1. Do not touch the person directly
  2. Turn off power at the breaker
  3. Use a non-conductive object to separate person from source
  4. Call 911 immediately
  5. Begin CPR if trained and necessary
  6. Seek immediate medical attention
