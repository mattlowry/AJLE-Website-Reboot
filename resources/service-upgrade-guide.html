Signs You Need a Service Upgrade

If you experience any of these warning signs, your electrical system may be outdated or inadequate for your needs.

Flickering Lights

Lights dim or flicker when major appliances like your air conditioner or microwave turn on, indicating insufficient power.

Limited Outlets

You rely heavily on power strips and extension cords because you don't have enough outlets for your devices.

Frequent Breaker Trips

Circuit breakers trip regularly, especially when multiple appliances are running simultaneously.

Old Electrical Panel

Your home has a fuse box or a panel that's more than 25 years old, which may not meet current safety standards.

Home Renovations

You're planning a major renovation, home addition, or installing high-powered appliances like an EV charger.

Hot Panel or Outlets

Your electrical panel, outlets, or switches feel warm to the touch, which can indicate dangerous overloading.

Service Upgrade Planning Timeline

Understanding the process and timeline for your electrical service upgrade helps you prepare for this important home improvement.

1

Initial Consultation (Day 1)

Schedule an assessment with a licensed electrician who will evaluate your current electrical system, discuss your needs, and provide a detailed quote.

What to prepare: List of electrical issues, future plans for home improvements, and questions about the upgrade process.
2

Permitting Process (Days 2-14)

Your electrician will submit the necessary permit applications to your local building department. This typically takes 1-2 weeks depending on your jurisdiction.

What to prepare: Property information, existing electrical documentation if available, and possibly HOA approval if required.
3

Utility Coordination (Days 7-21)

Your electrician will coordinate with your local utility company to schedule a temporary power disconnection. This step often runs concurrently with permitting.

What to prepare: Account information for your utility provider and potentially a temporary power plan if needed.
4

Installation Day (Day 22-23)

The actual service upgrade typically takes 1-2 days. Your power will be disconnected for 4-8 hours during installation of the new panel, service entrance cable, and meter base.

What to prepare: Clear access to electrical panel, backup plan for refrigerator items, and alternate arrangements if you work from home.
5

Inspection & Power Restoration (Day 23-24)

After installation, the work must be inspected by your local building department. Once approved, your utility company will reconnect your permanent power service.

Note: AJ Long Electric handles all inspection coordination and paperwork for you.

Total Timeline: Approximately 3-4 Weeks

Service Upgrade Cost Factors

Several factors influence the total cost of a service upgrade. Understanding these can help you better plan your budget.

Standard Inclusions

  • New 200 Amp Panel: Modern circuit breaker panel with more spaces
  • Service Entrance Cable: Heavy-duty wiring from utility connection to panel
  • Meter Base: Updated meter housing to accommodate increased capacity
  • Grounding System: Updated ground rods and connections
  • Labor: Professional installation by licensed electricians
  • Permits: All required permits and inspections

Additional Cost Factors

  • Panel Location Change: Moving your panel to a new location requires additional wiring and wall repairs
  • Service Type: Overhead vs. underground service connections have different requirements
  • Circuit Upgrades: Adding or upgrading specific circuits beyond the main panel
  • AFCI/GFCI Protection: Adding modern safety protection to circuits
  • Home Age: Older homes may require additional updates to meet current code
  • Structural Challenges: Difficult access or unique structural requirements

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about electrical service upgrades answered by our expert electricians.

How long will I be without power during the upgrade?

The power outage typically lasts 4-8 hours during the actual panel replacement. We coordinate with your utility company to minimize downtime and schedule the outage at a convenient time for your household.

Do I really need 200 amps? Would 150 amps be sufficient?

While 150 amps might be adequate for some smaller homes, 200 amps has become the standard for modern households. The cost difference between 150 and 200 amp upgrades is relatively minor, but 200 amps provides greater capacity for future needs, including EV charging, home additions, and new appliances.

Will a service upgrade require changes to my interior walls?

In most cases, we can replace your panel with minimal disruption to your walls. If your panel is being relocated or if you have an older home with outdated wiring, some wall repairs might be necessary. Our electricians take care to minimize damage and leave your home clean and tidy.

Is a permit required for a service upgrade?

Yes, electrical service upgrades always require permits and inspections. AJ Long Electric handles all permitting and inspection coordination as part of our service. This ensures your upgrade is properly documented and meets all local code requirements.

Will my homeowner's insurance be affected by a service upgrade?

In most cases, upgrading your electrical service positively impacts your homeowner's insurance. Many insurance companies offer discounts for homes with updated electrical systems as they pose less fire risk. We provide documentation of your upgrade that you can submit to your insurance company.

What's the difference between a panel replacement and a service upgrade?

A panel replacement simply swaps out your existing electrical panel with a new one of the same amperage, while a service upgrade increases your electrical capacity (typically from 100 to 200 amps). A service upgrade includes a new panel, service entrance cable, meter base, and coordination with your utility company.
