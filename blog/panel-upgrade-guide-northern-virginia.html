Complete Guide to Electrical Panel Upgrades in Northern Virginia

Everything you need to know about upgrading your electrical panel safely and legally

January 20, 2025 12 min read AJ Long Electric

Important Safety Notice

Electrical panel work requires permits and must be performed by licensed electricians in Virginia. Never attempt panel upgrades as a DIY project.

Your home's electrical panel is the heart of your electrical system, distributing power safely throughout your property. In Northern Virginia's growing communities, many homes still have outdated panels that can't handle modern electrical demands. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about electrical panel upgrades.

Signs You Need a Panel Upgrade

Age and Type of Current Panel

  • Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) panels: Installed 1950s-1980s, known fire hazard
  • Zinsco panels: Manufactured 1960s-1970s, frequent breaker failures
  • Fuse boxes: Any home still using fuses needs immediate upgrade
  • 60-amp or 100-amp service: Insufficient for modern homes

Performance Issues

Warning Signs

  • • Frequent breaker trips
  • • Flickering lights when appliances start
  • • Burning smell from panel
  • • Warm or hot panel cover
  • • Rust or corrosion visible

Capacity Issues

  • • Cannot add new circuits
  • • Running out of breaker spaces
  • • Planning major additions
  • • Installing EV charger
  • • Adding pool equipment

Modern panels from trusted manufacturers like Square D, Eaton, and GE offer superior safety and reliability compared to older brands. These panels include advanced safety features like arc fault and ground fault protection.

Virginia Permits and Code Requirements

Required Permits

Fairfax County Requirements

  • • Electrical permit required for all panel upgrades
  • • Licensed electrician must pull permit
  • • Inspection required before energizing
  • • Utility coordination for service upgrades

NEC Code Compliance

All panel upgrades must meet current National Electrical Code (NEC) standards, including:

  • AFCI Protection: Arc fault circuit interrupters for bedrooms and living areas
  • GFCI Protection: Ground fault protection for bathrooms, kitchens, outdoors
  • Surge Protection: Whole-house surge protectors recommended
  • Proper Grounding: Updated grounding and bonding systems
