Electrical Safety

What are signs that I might need an electrical panel upgrade?

Here are common signs that indicate you may need a panel upgrade:

  • Frequent circuit breaker trips
  • Flickering or dimming lights when appliances turn on
  • Need to unplug one device to use another
  • Still using a fuse box instead of circuit breakers
  • Buzzing sounds coming from the electrical panel
  • Warm or discolored outlets or switch plates
  • Home is over 25 years old with the original panel
  • Panel is less than 100 amps (most modern homes need 200A or more)
  • Adding major electrical loads (EV charger, hot tub, etc.)
  • Panel manufacturer is Federal Pacific Electric, Zinsco, or Challenger (known safety issues)

If you're experiencing any of these issues, we recommend scheduling an electrical inspection to assess your panel. Read our complete panel upgrade guide.

What should I do if I experience an electrical emergency?

In case of an electrical emergency:

  • If there's a fire: Evacuate immediately and call 911. Only use a fire extinguisher if the fire is small, contained, and you have a proper Class C extinguisher for electrical fires.
  • If someone is receiving an electrical shock: Do NOT touch them directly. Turn off power at the breaker or unplug the device if safe to do so. Call 911 immediately.
  • For burning odors, smoke, or sparks: Turn off power to the affected area at the main panel if safe to do so, and call us for emergency service.
  • For power outages affecting only your home: Check your main breaker first. If it's not tripped and your neighbors have power, call us.
  • For neighborhood power outages: Contact your utility company and follow their safety guidelines.

For electrical emergencies requiring professional help, call us at 703-997-0026 and follow the emergency prompts. Keep our number and your utility company's emergency number readily accessible.

How often should I have my home's electrical system inspected?

We recommend regular electrical inspections based on the age of your home:

  • Homes less than 10 years old: Every 5-7 years
  • Homes 10-25 years old: Every 3-5 years
  • Homes 25-40 years old: Every 2-3 years
  • Homes over 40 years old: Annually

You should also consider an inspection:

  • Before purchasing a home
  • After a major storm or flood
  • Before and after major renovations
  • When adding significant new electrical loads (like an EV charger)
  • If you notice flickering lights, tripping breakers, or other electrical issues

Regular inspections help identify potential issues before they become dangerous or costly problems. Contact us to schedule a comprehensive electrical safety inspection.
