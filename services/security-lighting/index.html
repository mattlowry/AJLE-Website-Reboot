Strategic Security Lighting Placement

Key Areas to Illuminate

  • Entry Points: All doors, windows, and potential access points
  • Driveways & Garages: Vehicle areas and garage doors
  • Walkways: All paths and stairs for safety and security
  • Dark Areas: Corners, landscaping, and blind spots
  • Outbuildings: Sheds, workshops, and detached structures

Design Considerations

  • Coverage Overlap: Eliminate dark spots between lights
  • Glare Control: Proper positioning to avoid blinding
  • Neighbor Consideration: Minimize light trespass
  • Power Access: Strategic electrical placement
  • Control Systems: Central control options

Frequently Asked Questions

How bright should security lights be?

Security lights should be bright enough to clearly illuminate the area (typically 700-1300 lumens) without creating glare or disturbing neighbors.

Do motion sensors work in all weather?

Quality motion sensors are weatherproof and work in all conditions, though sensitivity may need adjustment for extreme temperatures or weather.

Can security lights be on a timer?

Yes, we can install timer controls, photocells, or smart systems that allow custom scheduling and automatic operation of your security lights.
