Proper Attic Fan Sizing

Sizing Calculations

Proper fan sizing is crucial for effective attic ventilation. We calculate the required CFM (cubic feet per minute) based on:

  • Attic square footage
  • Roof pitch and height
  • Existing ventilation
  • Climate considerations
  • Home insulation levels

Common Sizing Guidelines

General recommendations for attic fan capacity based on attic size:

  • 1,000 sq ft: 1,000-1,500 CFM
  • 1,500 sq ft: 1,500-2,250 CFM
  • 2,000 sq ft: 2,000-3,000 CFM
  • 2,500 sq ft: 2,500-3,750 CFM
  • 3,000+ sq ft: Custom calculation

Frequently Asked Questions

Will an attic fan really lower my cooling costs?

Yes, by reducing attic temperatures from 150°F+ to near outside temperature, attic fans significantly reduce the heat load on your home and AC system, typically saving 10-30% on cooling costs.

Solar vs electric attic fans - which is better?

Both are effective. Solar fans have zero operating costs but depend on sunlight. Electric fans can run anytime but use electricity. We help you choose based on your specific needs and roof exposure.

Do I need additional roof vents with an attic fan?

Yes, attic fans require adequate intake ventilation (soffit vents) to work properly. We assess and can install additional vents if needed for balanced ventilation.
