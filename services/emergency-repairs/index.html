Common Electrical Emergencies

Electrical Fires

Sparks, burning smells, or visible flames from electrical equipment

Power Outages

Complete or partial loss of electrical power to your property

Shock Hazards

Exposed wires, wet electrical equipment, or shock risks

What to Do in an Electrical Emergency

Immediate Safety Steps

  • • Turn off power at the main breaker if safe to do so
  • • Never touch electrical equipment if you smell burning
  • • If someone is being shocked, turn off power before helping
  • • Evacuate if you see or smell electrical fires
  • • Call 911 for active fires or immediate danger

When to Call Emergency Service

  • • Burning smell from outlets or panels
  • • Sparks from electrical equipment
  • • Complete power loss (not weather-related)
  • • Electrical shock injuries
  • • Hot electrical panels or outlets
