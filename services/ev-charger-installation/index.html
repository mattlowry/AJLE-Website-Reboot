Home charging is the most convenient way to keep your electric vehicle ready to go. Our professional installation ensures your charger operates safely, efficiently, and reliably.

Why Professional EV Charger Installation?

While Level 1 chargers (that plug into standard outlets) are convenient, they're extremely slow, adding only about 3-5 miles of range per hour. Level 2 chargers can add 25-30 miles of range per hour, but require professional installation to ensure safety and code compliance.

Benefits of Professional Installation

Why choose professional EV charger installation over DIY approaches

Safety Assurance

EV chargers draw significant power and need proper installation to prevent electrical hazards. Our licensed electricians ensure all connections are secure and protected with appropriate circuit breakers.

Code Compliance

We handle all permitting requirements and ensure your installation meets National Electrical Code (NEC) standards and local regulations, which is essential for insurance coverage and home resale.

Optimal Performance

Professional installation ensures your charger operates at maximum efficiency, with proper wire sizing and circuit capacity to deliver the fastest possible charging speeds for your EV.

Electrical System Assessment

We evaluate your home's electrical system to determine if panel upgrades or additional work is needed to support your EV charger, preventing overloads and ensuring reliable operation.

Warranty Protection

DIY installations often void manufacturer warranties. Our professional installation maintains your charger's warranty coverage and comes with our own 5-year workmanship warranty.

Vehicle Protection

Properly installed chargers protect your vehicle's expensive battery systems from power surges and other electrical issues that could cause damage over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about EV charger installation

What's the difference between Level 1 and Level 2 EV charging?

Level 1 charging uses a standard 120V household outlet and typically adds just 3-5 miles of range per hour of charging. This is extremely slow and may not fully charge your vehicle overnight. Level 2 charging uses 240V power (like your dryer or oven) and can add 25-30+ miles of range per hour, allowing for a full charge overnight. Level 2 requires professional installation but is the recommended solution for EV owners who drive regularly.

Do I need to upgrade my electrical panel for an EV charger?

Not necessarily, but it depends on your home's existing electrical capacity. A Level 2 charger requires a dedicated 40-50 amp circuit. If your panel has available capacity and space for a new circuit breaker, you may not need an upgrade. However, older homes or panels that are already near capacity may require an upgrade. During our assessment, we'll evaluate your electrical panel and determine if an upgrade is needed. Approximately 30% of our installations require some form of panel upgrade or load management solution.

Can I install an EV charger in my garage/outdoors?

Yes, EV chargers can be installed both indoors (garage, carport) and outdoors. For outdoor installations, we use weather-resistant, UL-listed chargers and weatherproof enclosures to ensure safety and longevity. The location needs to be within reach of your vehicle's charging port when parked and ideally close to your electrical panel to minimize wiring costs. During our consultation, we'll recommend the optimal mounting location based on your specific property layout, vehicle type, and electrical system.

How long does EV charger installation take?

The actual installation typically takes 3-6 hours for standard installations, depending on complexity. This includes mounting the charger, running conduit and wiring, installing the circuit breaker, and testing the system. However, the complete process including scheduling, permitting, and final inspection can take 1-2 weeks from start to finish. Installations requiring panel upgrades may take longer. We'll provide you with a specific timeline during your consultation based on your installation needs and local permitting requirements.

Are there tax credits or rebates available for EV charger installation?

Yes, there are often financial incentives available. The federal Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit currently offers up to 30% of the cost of EV charging equipment and installation (up to $1,000). Additionally, many states, local utilities, and municipalities offer their own rebates or incentives. During our consultation, we can provide information about available incentives in your area and provide documentation needed for claiming these benefits. These incentives can significantly reduce the overall cost of your installation.

Do I need to buy my charger through you?

No, you don't have to purchase your charger through us. We offer flexible options:

1. Customer-supplied charger: If you've already purchased a charger or prefer to buy it yourself, we're happy to install it.

2. AJ Long Electric supplied charger: We can source and provide quality chargers at competitive prices as part of your installation package.

3. Recommendation only: We can recommend the best charger for your specific EV model and usage patterns, and you can purchase it separately.

What's most important is that the charger meets safety standards and is appropriate for your electrical system and vehicle.
