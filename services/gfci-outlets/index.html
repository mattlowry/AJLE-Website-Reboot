What are GFCI Outlets?

Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) outlets are specialized electrical safety devices that protect against electrical shock by detecting ground faults and automatically shutting off power in milliseconds.

  • Prevent electrical shock and electrocution
  • Detect ground faults in 1/40th of a second
  • Required by electrical code in wet areas
  • Easy to test and reset with built-in buttons

Where GFCI Outlets Are Required

Bathrooms

All bathroom outlets must be GFCI protected. Water and electricity don't mix - GFCI outlets provide essential protection.

Kitchen Countertops

All countertop outlets within 6 feet of sinks must be GFCI protected for safety near water sources.

Outdoor Areas

All outdoor outlets and those in garages, basements, and crawl spaces require GFCI protection.

Laundry Areas

Washing machine outlets and utility room receptacles must have GFCI protection.

Pool & Spa Areas

All outlets near pools, hot tubs, and spas require GFCI protection for water safety.

Unfinished Basements

Basement outlets and those in damp locations must be GFCI protected per electrical code.
