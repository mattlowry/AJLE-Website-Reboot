When to Schedule Electrical Maintenance

Warning Signs

  • Flickering or dimming lights
  • Frequent circuit breaker trips
  • Burning smells from outlets or panels
  • Warm or hot outlet covers and switches
  • Crackling or buzzing sounds
  • Electrical shocks from appliances

Recommended Schedule

  • Annual: Professional electrical inspection
  • Every 3 Years: Comprehensive system evaluation
  • Every 5 Years: Panel and main service inspection
  • Before Sale: Pre-listing electrical inspection
  • After Purchase: New home electrical assessment
  • After Storms: Weather damage assessment

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have electrical maintenance?

We recommend annual safety inspections for most homes, with comprehensive maintenance every 3-5 years depending on the age and condition of your electrical system.

What's included in an electrical inspection?

Our inspections cover electrical panels, circuits, outlets, switches, GFCI devices, grounding systems, and safety compliance with current electrical codes.

Can electrical maintenance prevent power outages?

While we can't prevent utility outages, regular maintenance helps prevent internal electrical failures that could cause power loss to parts of your home.
