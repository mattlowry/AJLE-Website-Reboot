Safety First

When to Call for Emergency Service

Complete Power Loss

Sudden loss of power to your entire home or specific areas when neighbors have power.

  • • Main breaker failures
  • • Service line issues
  • • Panel malfunctions
  • • Meter problems

Burning Smells

Acrid burning odors from outlets, switches, or electrical panels require immediate attention.

  • • Overheating wires
  • • Melting insulation
  • • Electrical fires
  • • Component failure

Sparking or Arcing

Visible sparks, electrical arcing, or buzzing sounds from electrical components.

  • • Outlet sparking
  • • Panel arcing
  • • Switch failures
  • • Wire damage

Electrical Shock

Receiving shocks from appliances, switches, or outlets indicates dangerous electrical faults.

  • • Grounding issues
  • • Faulty wiring
  • • Equipment failure
  • • Water damage

Water & Electrical

Water near electrical systems from flooding, leaks, or storms creates immediate danger.

  • • Flooded panels
  • • Wet outlets
  • • Storm damage
  • • Plumbing leaks

Storm Damage

Electrical damage from severe weather including lightning strikes and fallen power lines.

  • • Lightning strikes
  • • Downed lines
  • • Tree damage
  • • Surge damage

Emergency Electrical Safety Tips

Immediate Actions

  • Turn Off Power: Shut off main breaker if safe to do so
  • Stay Clear: Keep away from sparking or damaged electrical components
  • No Water: Never use water on electrical fires
  • Call 911: For fires or immediate danger

What NOT to Do

  • Don't Touch: Avoid contact with wet electrical equipment
  • Don't DIY: Never attempt emergency electrical repairs yourself
  • Don't Ignore: Small problems can become dangerous quickly
  • Don't Wait: Call for help immediately
