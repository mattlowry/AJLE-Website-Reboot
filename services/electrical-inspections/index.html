Our Inspection Process

We follow a systematic approach to ensure nothing is missed during your electrical safety inspection

1

Initial Visual Assessment

Our certified electrician begins with a comprehensive visual inspection of your electrical panel, visible wiring, outlets, switches, and fixtures. We look for obvious safety concerns, improper installations, and signs of wear or damage throughout your Vienna, Fairfax, or McLean home.

Duration: 30-45 minutes Documented findings
2

Electrical Panel Inspection

We thoroughly examine your main electrical panel, checking for proper labeling, adequate capacity, secure connections, and appropriate overcurrent protection. This critical step often reveals potential fire hazards in older homes across Springfield and Arlington.

Duration: 45-60 minutes Advanced testing equipment
3

Outlet & Circuit Testing

Using specialized testing equipment, we check every accessible outlet for proper grounding, polarity, and GFCI function. We also test circuit loads to ensure they're not overloaded and identify any circuits that may need upgrading for modern electrical demands.

Duration: 60-90 minutes Every outlet tested
4

Safety System Verification

We test all safety devices including smoke detectors, carbon monoxide detectors, and any surge protection systems. We also verify proper grounding of your electrical system and check for arc fault circuit interrupters (AFCIs) where required by code.

Duration: 30-45 minutes Safety compliance check
5

Detailed Report & Consultation

We provide a comprehensive written report detailing all findings, including photos, priority recommendations, and cost estimates for any needed repairs. We'll walk you through the report and answer any questions about your home's electrical safety.

Duration: 30 minutes Detailed PDF report

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about electrical safety inspections

How often should I get an electrical safety inspection?

We recommend electrical safety inspections every 3-5 years for most homes. However, homes over 40 years old should be inspected annually. You should also schedule an inspection when buying or selling a home, after major renovations, if you notice electrical problems (flickering lights, warm outlets, frequent breaker trips), or before significant electrical upgrades.

What exactly is included in an electrical safety inspection?

Our comprehensive inspection covers: electrical panel evaluation, testing all outlets and switches, checking GFCI and AFCI protection, inspecting visible wiring, verifying proper grounding, testing smoke and carbon monoxide detectors, examining outdoor electrical components, and checking major appliance circuits. You'll receive a detailed written report with photos, findings, and recommendations.

How much does an electrical safety inspection cost?

Our standard electrical safety inspection starts at $250 for homes up to 2,000 sq ft. Larger homes (2,000-3,500 sq ft) are typically $350, which includes thermal imaging and a more comprehensive report. Premium inspections for large homes or commercial properties start at $500. We provide free estimates and transparent pricing before any work begins.

How long does an electrical inspection take?

A typical residential electrical inspection takes 2-4 hours, depending on the size of your home and the complexity of your electrical system. Smaller homes and condos may take just 2 hours, while larger homes with multiple panels or complex systems may require 4+ hours. We'll provide a time estimate when scheduling your inspection.

Will you fix problems found during the inspection?

The inspection itself is diagnostic only - we don't make repairs during the inspection. This ensures an unbiased assessment. However, we provide detailed cost estimates for any needed repairs in our report. If you choose to have us perform the repairs, we typically offer a discount on the repair work when bundled with the inspection. We can often schedule repairs immediately following the inspection if desired.

Do I need to be home during the inspection?

Yes, we require an adult (18+) to be present during the inspection for security and access reasons. This also allows you to ask questions and learn about your electrical system. If you can't be present, a trusted adult representative can stand in for you. We'll walk through our findings at the end of the inspection and answer any questions you have.
