Motion Sensor Technologies

We install various motion sensor technologies, each optimized for specific applications and environments.

PIR Sensors

Passive infrared sensors detect body heat changes, ideal for detecting human movement while ignoring small animals.

Range: 10-40 feet

Best for: Entry points, walkways

Microwave Sensors

Emit microwaves and detect movement through reflection changes. Wide coverage area with high sensitivity.

Range: Up to 100 feet

Best for: Large areas, garages

Dual Technology

Combines PIR and microwave for maximum accuracy and reduced false triggering from weather or animals.

Range: 15-60 feet

Best for: Security applications

Camera-Based

Video analytics detect human shapes and movement patterns with advanced AI filtering capabilities.

Range: Variable field of view

Best for: Smart security systems

Ultrasonic Sensors

Emit high-frequency sound waves and detect movement through frequency changes. Works in any lighting condition.

Range: 6-20 feet

Best for: Indoor applications

Photocell Integration

Light sensors ensure motion activation only occurs in darkness, preventing daytime triggering.

Feature: Dusk-to-dawn operation

Best for: Outdoor security lighting

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about motion sensor lighting installation and performance.

How do I prevent false triggering from pets or wind?

Use PIR sensors with pet immunity features, adjust sensitivity settings appropriately, and position sensors to avoid areas where small animals typically travel. Dual-technology sensors combine PIR and microwave detection to reduce false triggers from wind-blown objects while maintaining sensitivity to human movement.

What's the typical range and coverage area for motion sensors?

PIR sensors typically detect motion 10-40 feet away with a 120-180 degree coverage angle. Microwave sensors can reach up to 100 feet but may be more sensitive to interference. Sensor placement height and angle significantly affect the detection pattern - our technicians optimize positioning for your specific security needs.

Can motion sensor lights work with LED bulbs and smart home systems?

Yes, modern motion sensors are fully compatible with LED bulbs and many integrate with smart home systems. LED compatibility reduces energy consumption further, while smart integration allows remote monitoring, scheduling, and automation. Some sensors can send notifications to your smartphone when triggered.

How much do motion sensor light installations typically cost?

Installation costs range from $150-$600 per location depending on the sensor type, fixture complexity, and electrical requirements. Simple replacement installations are less expensive than new installations requiring electrical circuits. We provide detailed estimates including equipment and labor for all projects.
