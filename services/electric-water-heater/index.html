Electrical Requirements

Tank Water Heaters

  • • 240V dedicated circuit
  • • 30-50 amp breaker
  • • 10-6 gauge wire
  • • 4500-5500 watt elements
  • • Disconnect switch required

Tankless Units

  • • Multiple 240V circuits
  • • 60-150 amp service
  • • Panel upgrade often needed
  • • 18-36 kW power draw
  • • Special breaker requirements

Safety Requirements

  • • Proper grounding
  • • GFCI protection
  • • Emergency shutoff
  • • Code compliance
  • • Permit & inspection

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do electric water heaters last?

Traditional tank water heaters typically last 10-15 years, while tankless units can last 20+ years with proper maintenance. Regular maintenance extends lifespan.

Is tankless worth the extra cost?

Tankless water heaters cost more upfront but save 30-50% on energy costs and last twice as long. They're ideal for smaller spaces and provide endless hot water.

Do I need a permit for water heater replacement?

Yes, most jurisdictions require permits for water heater installation or replacement. We handle all permit applications and coordinate required inspections.
